Welcome,

I am an enthusiastic, motivated and happy-go-lucky web developer loving life in Wellington, NZ.

I love... being part of a creative team, coming up with innovative solutions to every day problems. I am well versed with the build-measure-learn / agile approach to development, no stranger to hard work or long hours and will always go the extra mile to ensure success.

In my free time... you will find me hanging out with my family, enjoying delicious food and taking our dog for long walks in the great outdoors.

It all began...... in early 2015, when I made the decision to leave a successful career in the hospitality industry to co-found a tech startup, with the vision of providing cafes, bars and restaurants with something they often lack, but so desperately need; honest, private and direct customer feedback.

I recruited 2 co-founders and together we created MightyGem.com and had the distinction of being selected from over 200 applicants as one of 10 teams to partake in Lightning Lab, Christchurch 2015 - New Zealand's premier business accelerator program for high-growth digital startups. It was here a found my passion for web development.

Between January and June, 2016, I worked hard to learn the basics of web development using online resources (Codecademy, Treehouse, freeCodeCamp) before enrolling with Enspiral Dev Academy, Wellington. At EDA, I arrived 2-3 hours early each day to recap on the previous day and prepare for the current day's learnings. It was this extra commitment, dedication and drive which saw me move from one of the greenest in the class to one of the most proficient. By the final cohort project, I had a solid understanding of the JS, React & Redux stack and was appointed the project manager, team leader and product owner to help lead 6 new devs to victory.

I gained employment on the day I graduated from EDA and have been working in tech ever since. I love the work I do and continue to put time aside each day to grow bigger, better, faster, stronger through online tutorials and publications.

Thanks for listening,
